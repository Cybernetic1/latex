\section{Economics of the Solidi Coin \\}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Exponential Conversion.}  A member can spend money to ``pump up'' his reputation.  As reputation gets higher, it is increasingly harder to increase one unit of reputation, following an \textbf{exponential} curve: $e^{kR}$ is required to increase one unit of $R$, where $R$ is reputation, $k$ is a scaling constant, eg. $1/1000$.
	
	\item \textbf{Withdrawals} can be performed as the inverse conversion of the above.  But this will be problematic; see below.
	
	\item \textbf{Generosity of Likes.}  Every time a member likes a comment, the creator of the comment get +1 unit reputation, while the sender gets $\frac{1}{100}$ deduction in reputation, ie 0.01 unit.
	
	\item \cc{
		由于 exponential conversion，当某会员得到了稍为可观的声誉，就很有可能 cash-out (withdraw)，获得现金，而导致平台因缺乏现金而崩盘。 }{
		Due to exponential conversion, when a member gets a considerable reputation, she may be tempted to cash-out (withdraw), causing the platform to run out of cash and collapse.  How to prevent this?
	}
	
	\item \cc{
		有个解决办法是： cash-out 是根据 整体 声誉 的比例 计算的。 当现金储备很少的时候，cash-out 微不足道。}{
		A solution is:  withdrawals are calculated as a \textbf{proportion} of the reputation pool, instead of exponential conversion.  This means that when the total cash reserve of the platform is low, withdrawals would also be insignificant.
	}
	
	
\end{itemize}
